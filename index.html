Biorobotics Research

Organic Robotics Lab at Cornell University in Ithaca, NY

I work on a project in the intersection of fungi and robotics. In the beginning stages of this project, I collected fungi electrical signals utilizing microscaled electrodes (Figure 1). Following the data collection, I cleaned the data from noise utilizing various Fourier filters. I analyze the amplitudes and frequencies of the cleaned fungi electrical signals and utilize these cleaned signals as inputs to supervised machine learning models. Furthermore, I use the raw fungi signals as input to unsupervised machine learning models. The signals analyzed are utilized to power a robotics system.

Figure 1. Fungi electrical signal collection from petri dish with fungi culture.

Biomedical Imaging

MRI Lab at Weill Cornell Medicine in NYC

I work on a project in which I utilize classical machine learning models to diagnose neurodegenerative diseases based on T1W and QSM MRI Scans (Figure 2). Prior to applying the machine learning to the MRI images, I normalized the MRI scans of 50+ subjects to a common template.
